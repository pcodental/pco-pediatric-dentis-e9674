Frequently Asked Questions About Braces

How much do braces cost at PCO Pediatric Dentistry & Orthodontics in New York, and do you accept insurance?
The cost of braces at our New York office typically ranges from $4,500 to $7,500, depending on the type of braces and complexity of treatment. We accept most major dental insurances and can help you check your specific coverage. Flexible payment plans are also available to make treatment more affordable for families.
How long does the braces process take, from the first consultation to removal?
For most patients, the entire braces process takes between 18 and 24 months. Your first consultation includes a thorough exam, x-rays, and a personalized treatment plan. We usually schedule the placement of braces within a week or two after the initial visit, and regular adjustments are done every 4–8 weeks. After braces are removed, a retainer is provided to maintain results.
Are your orthodontists licensed and experienced with children and teens?
Absolutely. All of our orthodontists are board-certified and licensed to practice in New York State, with specialized training in pediatric dentistry and orthodontics. Our team has years of experience working with children and adolescents, ensuring both effective treatment and a positive experience for young patients.
What does the braces process involve at your Manhattan office?
The process begins with a comprehensive consultation, including digital x-rays and photos. Once your custom treatment plan is ready, we schedule an appointment to place the braces, which takes about 1–2 hours. You'll return every 4–8 weeks for adjustments, and our staff is always available to answer questions or handle emergencies. We focus on patient comfort and clear communication throughout the process.
Do you offer any guarantees on orthodontic results or follow-up care?
We are committed to delivering high-quality results and provide complimentary follow-up visits for one year after braces removal to monitor your smile. While outcomes depend on following your orthodontist’s instructions (like wearing your retainer), we stand by our care and are always available if concerns arise after treatment.
