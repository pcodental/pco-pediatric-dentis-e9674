Frequently Asked Questions About Pediatric Preventive Dental Care

How much do pediatric dental check-ups cost at PCO Pediatric Dentistry & Orthodontics in New York City?
At PCO Pediatric Dentistry & Orthodontics, a standard pediatric dental check-up typically ranges from $150 to $200, depending on the specific services needed (such as cleanings, fluoride treatments, or digital X-rays). We accept most major insurance plans common in New York, and our front desk can assist you with verifying your coverage and any out-of-pocket expenses before your visit.
How soon can my child get an appointment for preventive dental care, and how long does a typical visit take?
We strive to offer appointments within 1-2 weeks, though same-week slots are sometimes available, especially for routine preventive care. A typical pediatric check-up and cleaning at our Manhattan office takes about 45 minutes to an hour. This allows time for the exam, cleaning, fluoride application, and answering any parent or child questions.
Are your pediatric dentists and orthodontists licensed and experienced to treat children?
Yes, all of our providers hold current New York State dental licenses and have completed specialized training in pediatric dentistry and orthodontics. Our team is experienced in working with children of all ages and backgrounds, including those with special needs, ensuring a safe and comfortable experience for every child.
What is the process for a child's first dental visit at your practice?
For your child's first visit to PCO Pediatric Dentistry & Orthodontics, we'll start with a gentle tour to help them feel at ease. The dentist will review your child's medical and dental history, perform a thorough oral exam, clean their teeth, and apply fluoride if appropriate. We'll also discuss age-specific home care tips and answer any questions you have about your child's oral health. If you have insurance, please bring your card and any relevant documents.
Do you guarantee the results of preventive care, such as cavity prevention, for children?
While our pediatric preventive care services are designed to significantly reduce the risk of cavities and other dental issues, we cannot guarantee that your child will never develop cavities. Oral health depends on many factors, including genetics and home care habits. However, we are committed to providing ongoing education, high-quality care, and regular monitoring to give your child the best possible protection against dental problems.
